# Starborn: Comprehensive Hub & World Design Audit

This design blueprint audits and details the gameplay loops, puzzles, acoustic themes, and cooking/rest stops for all **12 Hubs** across **Worlds 1 through 6**. It ensures that the gameplay is varied, mechanically rich, and tightly integrated with the core themes of **Acoustic Resonance** and **Neurological Erosion**.

---

## Thematic & Mechanical Pillars

### 1. Acoustic Soundscapes
Electronic synthesizers are phased out in favor of physical resonators (cello, singing bowls, pipe organs, chimes, glass).

### 2. Sensory Anchors (Cooking & Rest Stops)
Cooking meals around cookfires restores health and halts Erosion. These rest spots trigger intimate dialogues where characters recall memories—directly building the emotional stakes for Nova's eventual memory-loss sacrifice.

### 3. Tuning Puzzles
Replaces instant-solve hotspots with two formats:
*   **Wave-Tuning Slider Minigame:** A tactile overlay where players drag sliders (Frequency, Amplitude, Phase) to match a target wave.
*   **Dialogue Frequencies:** Choices in conversation where players must tune filters or choose the correct pitches based on local audio clues.

---

## Translating Classic JRPG Puzzles to Acoustic Resonance

To evoke nostalgia while maintaining *Starborn*'s unique theme, traditional JRPG dungeon puzzles are translated into acoustic and physical equivalents:

| Classic JRPG Puzzle | Starborn Resonance Equivalent | Mechanical Implementation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Push/Pull Blocks** | *Acoustic Shifting blocks* | Moving sound-dampening basalt pillars or resonating crystals to redirect sound wave beams from an emitter to a gate receiver. |
| **Switches / Levers** | *Tuning Pegs & Air Valves* | Turning giant tuning pegs or opening air-flow valves to adjust background pitch, aligning separate frequencies into a clean chord. |
| **Key & Locked Door** | *Tuning Forks & Lock Resonators* | Finding a tuned physical fork (e.g., a "320Hz Fork"). Tapping the lock with the matching fork opens the gate via sympathetic vibration. |
| **Password Telegraph** | *Melodic Echo / Wave Glyphs* | Deciphering code patterns telegraphed as musical rhythms (e.g., "three clangs and a hum") or sine wave murals carved on room walls. |

---

## Optional Secret Caches & Side-Quest Integration

To encourage exploration and reward curiosity:
*   **Optional Secret Caches:** Hidden containers locked behind optional puzzles, yielding rare gear mods, high-value components, or unique cooking ingredients.
*   **Side Quest Puzzles:** Puzzles that resolve non-essential neighborhood problems, unlocking special character story interactions and companion reputation.
*   **Character Field Actions (Rare & Limited):** Used as occasional narrative flavor rather than a clunky constant mechanic (e.g., Nova using her signature kick to knock a rusted lever loose, or Orion reading a light spectrum only he can see).

## World 1: Mining Colony (Old Songs of Iron)
*   **Narrative Tone:** Gritty, dry, rusted iron. Humanity surviving on old, heavy mechanical loops.

### **Hub 1: Homestead**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Awakening, navigating the residential quarters, repairing Jed's cryo-heater, gathering basic scrap.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Dusty acoustic slide guitar (blues). Sound of steel plates groaning and wind whistling through ventilation shafts.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Nova's Bunkroom (Introductory rest point; triggers a memory of Nova's childhood).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **Rusted Circuit Breaker.** Restoring power requires flipping breaker switches in a specific sequence to match the flickering generator lights.
*   **Optional Cache (Lock & Key):** **Jed's Old Toolbox.** Located in the scrap heap. Locked with a standard padlock. Finding the key in the ventilation shaft opens it to reward a `wiring_bundle` and a rare mod component.
*   **Character Action (Rare):** Nova kicks a jammed bulkhead door (`nova_hydraulic_kick`) to open a shortcut back to the bunkroom, establishing her physical character trait.

### **Hub 2: Logistics (Sector 4)**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Navigating the loading bays, servicing cargo bots, and escaping the facility lockdown.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Industrial blues. Heavy metallic stampers and pneumatic pistons acting as a percussion track beneath the slide guitar.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Sector 4 Mess Hall (Unlocks the basic *Ration Soup* cooking recipe using ration packs).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **"Heavy Lifting" Gate.** Dialogue pressure alignment (adjusting pressure to 60Hz) to open the Deep Elevator gate.
*   **Side Quest Puzzle:** **"Water Pipe Tuning."** A colony resident needs help with a vibrating pipe. The player turns three peg valves (Dialogue tuning) to stop the pipe's discordant vibration, preventing it from bursting and receiving a `portable_stove` upgrade component.

## World 2: The Wilds (Resonant Canopy)
*   **Narrative Tone:** Organic, overgrown Architect structures. Glow-water streams hum with mineral energy.

### **Hub 3: Sector 9 Stream**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Resonance fishing, harvesting wild flora, navigating jungle brush.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Forest breeze, rushing water ambient, wooden acoustic flutes, and string plucks.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Sheltered Cookfire Recess (Unlocks *Glowfish Broth* and *Spicy Ramen* cooking; Zeke talks about his favorite street food back home).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **Sympathetic Stasis Lock.** Zeke's stasis pod ring alignment. Nova must search the stream area for a mineral-dense chime stone that resonates at C#m7 (136.1Hz) and hold it near the lock.
*   **Optional Cache (Dialogue Tuning):** **Hollow Root Stash.** Tapping a hollow root wall reveals a vibrating echo. The player must whistle the matching pitch (Dialogue choice) to make the plant retract its roots, revealing a cache of rare lures.

### **Hub 4: Architect Ruins**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Exploring ancient chambers, restoring power manifolds, and rebooting Astra's core computer.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Low, resonant stone humming. Standing waves that hum louder as players approach power hubs.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Astra Bridge Rest Spot (Restoring focus before the ruins exploration).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **Resonator Deflection Chamber.** Slide two massive, sound-absorbing basalt blocks onto pressure plates to clear the standing wave path.
*   **Side Quest Puzzle:** **"Fauna Stabilizing."** A wild creature is trapped in a discordant resonance hum. Using Orion's frequency matching (2-slider Wave-Tuning) calms the creature so Zeke can treat its wounds.

## World 3: The Spire (Noir and Scholar Glass)
*   **Narrative Tone:** Heavy rainfall, towering concrete, a clean, sterile upper caste suppressing the lower levels.

### **Hub 5: Lower City / Sewers**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Evading scanning grids, traversing laundry vents, sneaking past patrols.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Sci-fi noir jazz. Muffled upright bass, slow brush drums, and the continuous pattern of rain dripping on metal pipes.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Underrail Hideout (Cookfire stop where the team rests and shares cooked meals while hiding from the Dominion scanner nets).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **Security Bypass.** The player gathers components to craft a *Signal Bypass Mod* (Tinkering) which acts as a fake electronic frequency keycard to bypass the sewer sensor grid.
*   **Side Quest Puzzle:** **"Draining the Alleys."** Bypassing local floodgates. Turn three rusty valve wheels to match the pattern of high/low steam indicators telegraphed on the nearby billboard.

### **Hub 6: Upper City / Archive**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Infiltrating the Scholar Archive, bypassing security fields, and retrieving the Prism Lens.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** High-frequency, crystalline sounds. Elegant grand piano, glass harmonica drones, and chimes.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Archive Reading Room (A quiet lounge where characters can brew hot tea and rest).
*   **Puzzle (Wave-Tuning Minigame):** **Prism Gallery Light Puzzle.** Aligning visible light waves. A 3-slider puzzle (Frequency, Amplitude, and Phase) to focus the Archive's containment field.
*   **Optional Cache (Tuning Fork Lock):** **Scholar's Private Safe.** Bypassed by finding a specific *440Hz Tuning Fork* hidden inside a museum display case and striking it against the safe. Yields a rare Focus-boosting accessory.

## World 4: The Foundry (Factory Rhythm)
*   **Narrative Tone:** Extreme heat, bubbling slag, relentless automation.

### **Hub 7: Slag Pits**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Traversing lava bridges, avoiding blue thermal bursts, and stabilizing equipment.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Deep industrial drone, bubbling liquid iron, and hissing steam vents.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Basalt Shoring Recess (Cool air pocket; resting halts the environmental Erosion buildup).
*   **Puzzle (Main Quest):** **Steam-Valve Melody.** Look at the pressure gauges on the wall (environmental password telegraph showing symbols for High, Medium, Low) and turn three steam valves to match that sequence to open the floodgates.
*   **Optional Cache (Conveyor Shunting):** **Melted Loot Crate.** Aligning slag valves to temporarily shut off a lava spillway, allowing access to a charred crate containing rare plating components.

### **Hub 8: Assembly Line / Forge**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Dodge stamping machines, navigate moving conveyor belts, and claim the Forge Anvil.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Heavy industrial rock/metal. Rhythmic, crushing stampers and sirens forming a fast-paced battle beat.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Recycler Breakroom (Shared food stop; characters express their weariness and fear of the core's intense pressure).
*   **Puzzle (Wave-Tuning Minigame):** **Conveyor Timing Puzzle.** Match the stamper's mechanical frequency via sliders to create a window of silence, allowing the party to pass safely.
*   **Side Quest Puzzle:** **"The Lost Anvil Schematic."** Decipher the repeating mechanical chime pattern (e.g., Short-Short-Long clangs) telegraphed by the furnace exhaust to open a schematic locker.

## World 5: The Void (Glass and Vacuum)
*   **Narrative Tone:** Decompression, cosmic silence, massive structures hanging in space.

### **Hub 9: Orbital Ring**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Managing oxygen seals, traversing low-gravity solar bridges, and shielding against solar storms.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Spatial ambient, muffled bass, glass hums, and cosmic wind.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Observation Dome (A serene spot overlooking the silent planet).
*   **Puzzle (Tinkering Puzzle):** **Gravity Emitter Calibration.** Crafting a *Gravity Damper Mod* in the Tinkering menu to navigate a broken gravity bridge.
*   **Optional Cache (Phase Alignment):** **Decompressed Locker.** Reorient three solar reflector pegs to align the light beams, powering up a decompression door.

### **Hub 10: Deep Ring / Core**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Approaching the solar containment shields, traversing deep reactor corridors.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Tension-filled classical piano, low hums, and the sound of vibrating hulls.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Solar Shield Vault (A reinforced bunker to rest and eat before the final reactor chamber).
*   **Puzzle (Wave-Tuning Minigame):** **Failsafe Decryption.** A complex 3-slider wave-matching puzzle to bypass the reactor's main core containment lock.

---

## World 6: The Source (The Grand Chorus)
*   **Narrative Tone:** The boundary of reality. The Ocean's waves are visible and audible. Fading memories.

### **Hub 11: Event Horizon**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Navigating a shattered reef of memories, reclaiming character fragments, and fighting echo battles.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Ethereal, modal choral singing. Whispered voices and wind chimes echoing from the reef.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** Memory Oasis (The **final shared meal**. A heavy narrative moment where Nova's dialogue options start to glitch and lose words, showing her memory loss).
*   **Puzzle (Dialogue & Wave Tuning):** **Echo Tuning.** Adjusting the pitch of the characters' memory echoes to anchor their physical forms back together.

### **Hub 12: Singularity**
*   **Gameplay Loop:** Traversing the final bridge into the Source, merging the Chorus, and executing the ending.
*   **Acoustic Theme:** Grand orchestral choir. Rising crescendos that suddenly cut to silence, representing lost memories.
*   **Sensory Rest Stop:** None (The point of no return).
*   **Puzzle (Wave-Tuning Minigame):** **The Harmonic Choir Lock.** The final puzzle. The sliders match Nova's own heartbeat frequency, requiring perfect alignment as the UI text fades and she sacrifices her memories to save her friends.
