# Starborn — Master Story Bible

**Status:** Canon Skeleton (Refined Jan 2026)
**Tone:** JRPG-leaning Sci-Fi. Humor vs. Cosmic Horror. Found Family.

**Authority:** This document governs plot, campaign causality, antagonist plans, world transitions, and the ending. When sources conflict, use this order: Master Story Bible, Lore Bible, Character Arcs, Writer's Handbook, supporting world/hub documents, then production assets. `Characters.md` and `Emotional_and_Conflict_Map.md` are supporting summaries.

## Canon locks

- Nova is a 22-year-old human miner and mechanic with no Aethel ancestry, genetic compatibility, chosen status, prophecy, or prior Chime exposure.
- Her ungoverned cutter wakes the Tuning Fork through a buried conduit. She later counter-tunes it through a cutter-suit-operator calibration loop at 87 kHz phase sweep, 68% cold loop, and 180 degrees ground phase.
- The Fork handshake writes an Architect protocol signature into Nova's nervous system. The Cryo-Inductor fuses; only the Flux Liner's sacrificial ground strip is spent, and the armor remains equipped.
- The Chime is Orion's identity key, paired beacon, and imprint carrier. It supplies no sustained power, fuel, thrust, weapon effect, Bridge startup current, or Shield bypass.
- The Bridge is separate Architect Echo #2. It connects distinct willing minds without merging them.
- Architects made the six Echoes and exploited Aethel consciousness. The Aethel survived, adapted, preserved, hid, and operated some Architect systems; they did not create the Echoes.
- Gh0st is a distinct person built around a partial derivative of Elara's neural map, without her memories or identity. An Elara-calibrated Source Lock pauses one lethal action and issues no protective command.
- Routine Source use costs pain, fatigue, sensory crossover, signal lag, lost perceived seconds, and neurological stability. Memory is never routine fuel.
- World 2 travel is low-altitude to the Spire. World 3 crosses an internal curtain to the Foundry. Lens, Engine, and Arrays breach the outer Shield in Worlds 4-5.
- The finale burns out Nova's Source access while preserving memory and identity. Vale and Harmony fall locally; a regional opening forms, and the wider Dominion survives.

# I. High-Level Premise

Starborn follows **Nova**, a scavenger in a Dominion mining colony, who uncovers an ancient Echo Relic that leaves her **Echo-marked**—able to interact with **the Source**, the psionic frequency of the universe. The Dominion Corporation, led by **Director Mara Thorne**, is mining the planet not for ore, but to excavate these artifacts and turn the Source into a monopoly: patents, lock-ins, and a labor world that never stops paying.

Thorne sells the project as “Harmony,” but her motive is ownership: margin, leverage, and a Director’s seat that never has to answer to a board. The Dominion already builds Source-harnessing tech (Spectral ID locks, relay augmentation, phase-curtain corridor control); the Echoes are the capstone that turns a portfolio into root access.

Her fixer, **Lieutenant Arden Vale**, is worse—not greedy, but convinced: a radical idealist who believes the only way to end suffering is to erase the fracture of self.

Nova must assemble a crew of broken outcasts, travel across distinct biomes (Worlds), and stop **Ascension**—Vale’s Soloist reboot of the Great Silence: a reality rewrite that erases personhood.

## IIa. Progressive Campaign Playtime & Scaling Blueprint

To achieve a cohesive **12–15 hour total game length** without making the content feel front-loaded or bloated, the campaign follows a progressive scaling curve. Later worlds expand in physical room scale and mechanical complexity as the party gathers relics and upgrades *The Astra*.

### Campaign Target Matrix

| Seq. | World/Chapter | Target Playtime | Room Target | NPC Density | Relic / Mechanic Focus |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **The Mines** | **1.5 – 2.0 hrs** | 81 rooms (Actual) | High (~8 NPCs) | Onboarding, Hacking, Guard Break |
| **-** | **The Crash** | **0.25 hrs** | Interlude | N/A | Escaping, Splicing the Chime |
| **2** | **The Wilds** | **2.0 – 2.5 hrs** | 91 rooms (Actual) | Low (2 new NPCs) | Camping, Cooking, Tinkering, Erosion |
| **3** | **The Spire** | **2.5 – 3.0 hrs** | ~100–110 rooms | High (~8-10 NPCs) | Lens Relic (Scan), Grid Phase Heist |
| **4** | **The Foundry** | **2.5 – 3.0 hrs** | ~100–110 rooms | Low (0-2 NPCs) | Anvil Relic (Construct), Verticality |
| **5** | **The Void** | **3.0 – 4.0 hrs** | ~120–130 rooms | Medium (~4 NPCs) | Station Infiltration, Relic Integration |
| **6** | **The Source** | **1.5 – 2.0 hrs** | ~40–50 rooms | Zero (Core Party) | Climax, Living Circuit, Final Boss |
| **Total**| **Campaign** | **13.0 – 16.5 hrs**| **~530–590 rooms**| **Balanced Hubs** | **Phase Progression Spine** |

### World-by-World Pacing Strategy

1. **World 1 (Stellarium Colony 4):** Linear corridors and corporate offices. High density of social dialogue and tutorial messaging to ground the player.
2. **World 2 (Sector 9):** Abandoned wilderness and overgrown ruins. Pacing slows down to emphasize survival, camping, and rebuilding *The Astra*. A sparse NPC count emphasizes isolation.
3. **World 3 (Sector 0):** Dense, vertical neon city. Returns to high NPC density (bars, streets, shops) to make the urban heist feel populated and dangerous. Traversal requires scanning phase lines.
4. **World 4 (The Foundry):** Industrial forge built into geo-thermal magma vents. A combat-heavy dungeon crawl focusing on environmental hazards and construct path generation.
5. **World 5 (Orbital Ring):** A massive space station. Highly complex layout requiring the player to merge all previous mechanics to disable sector firewalls.
6. **World 6 (The Source):** A surreal, focused final ascent. Neurological strain threatens the crew's ability to keep their practiced circuit stable.

# III. Detailed World Outlines

## PROLOGUE: THE SIGNAL (Cold Open)
*   **Setting:** Aethel–Dominion Joint Facility, Sector 9.
*   Clean lab lighting. Fresh steel bolted onto ancient stone. Dominion warning stripes over Architect geometry.
*   Dominion consoles spit graphs and warnings: the Source is spiking.
*   Red strobes. A siren that sounds like it’s chewing through the air.
*   **Lab PA:** "SOURCE BEAST CONTAINMENT BREACH."
*   The far doors bow inward like something is leaning on them from the other side—geometry warping, too big for the room.
*   Orion rips a **Chime** (Ghost Signal Cell) from its cradle: a Source resonator keyed to his signature.
*   Orion’s voice is a hiss meant for machines and prayers. "Get the signal out. If Veyra can hear me, it can find me."
*   He slams the Chime into the launch tube and hits the release. It rockets away into darkness.
*   For a single impossible beat, the Chorus answers—not a voice, but a soft chord like many notes agreeing to carry it.
*   Orion dives into stasis. "Mute my signal. Now."
*   The glass seals. A shadow lunges at the pod—then cut to black.

## World 1: The Mines (Stellarium Colony 4)
*   **The Status Quo:** Nova is an indentured "Asset" (Scavenger) in debt to the Dominion. She is desperate to meet her quota.
*   **The Hook (The Hustle):** Four hours of sleep, one ration, absence penalties, and scrip debt leave Nova short of quota. She deliberately removes the cutter governor because factory specification cannot keep pace. Jed rebuilds the Cryo-Inductor and patches the suit-linked Flux Liner. Nova grounds the liner, explicitly confirms the bypass, and runs a live test.
*   **The Mechanical Cause:** The prohibited cutter sweep enters a buried Architect conduit, blows a sector breaker, wakes the dormant Tuning Fork, and cracks its failing chamber seal. The terminal logs Nova's operator ID while the damage report retains her cutter ID.
*   **The Punishment:** Foreman Bogs catches her. As punishment, he sends her to **Sector 4** (The Dead End) to "clear the magnetic interference" that has been messing with the sensors. "You like high voltage? Go fix that."
*   **The Twist (Zeke):** The logged fault flags Nova as **"Asset Liability: High."** Zeke changes the classification enough to prevent immediate recycling, but the operator/cutter record survives.
*   **The Discovery:** Boggs sends Nova to Sector 4 to clear the interference she caused. The Fork repeats the cutter pulse and destabilizes the chamber. Nova recognizes harmonic feedback and counter-tunes it at **87 kHz phase sweep, 68% cold loop, and 180 degrees ground phase**.
*   **The Handshake:** The cradle reads the cutter-suit-operator loop as a calibration rig. The Cryo-Inductor absorbs the thermal spike and fuses. The Flux Liner sends the discharge into the chamber floor; only its sacrificial strip is spent. Nova keeps the armor. The Fork writes an Architect protocol mark into her nervous system. Pain, tinnitus, tremor, sensory crossover, and a half-second suit-clock gap follow. Her identity and memories remain intact.
*   **The Climax:** Nova flees to Jed. He gives her the salvaged **"Ghost Signal Cell"** because old launch systems recognize Orion's authorization signature. The pod has its own power. Jed stays behind and holds the failing blast doors so she can launch.
*   **The Cost:** Nova’s first uncontrolled Source flare leaves her shaky and disoriented—and pings Dominion sensors hard enough to trigger the purge response.

## Interlude: The Crash
*   **The Hook:** The pod clears the rail on its own power with the Chime spliced into its authorization/navigation bus.
*   **The Twist:** The nav hard-locks onto a signal in Sector 9. Zeke can't override it.
*   **The Explanation:** They hit the Planetary Shield outside an authorized corridor. The escape becomes a physics problem, not a fight.
*   **The Climax:** The Planetary Shield flares. The pod slams into the upper canopy and spirals into the Wilds.

## World 2: The Wilds (Sector 9)
*   **The Hook:** Nova and Zeke crash. They are lost.
*   **Recruiting Orion:** The Chime authenticates Orion at the Temple Gate. Sanctuary reserve cells wake the facility and release him from stasis. He recognizes the identity beacon he launched **twenty years ago**.
*   **Finding The Astra:** The party discovers a crashed Dominion survey ship (The Astra) nearby. It is a wreck, but salvageable.
*   **The Bridge:** The crew recovers Architect Echo #2 from a sealed Sanctuary cradle. It is separate from the Chime and from the optional Bridge calibration record.
*   **Recruiting Gh0st:** At Canopy Ridge, Nova's Fork mark trips an Elara-calibrated Source Lock and pauses one lethal action. The failsafe issues no command. When the Source Beast attacks, Gh0st freely turns his weapon toward it and protects the party.
*   **The Anchor Drill:** Only after the Beast falls, Orion runs a grounded Link exercise. Zeke clips a lead to Nova's bracer, Gh0st voluntarily offers his armored hand, and Orion holds the counter-tone. Link opens between distinct willing minds. The Hunter closes and Link unlocks only after the drill.
*   **The Climax:** They mount the separate Bridge Echo in *The Astra*, power it with salvaged conduits and ship current, and use the Chime only to authenticate Orion. The repaired ship follows a low-altitude service airlane to the Spire.
*   **The Cost:** Nova learns fast that Source power isn’t free—Link and Blast burn her out on fumes until Orion forces “rest, focus, practice” into the plan.
*   **Breathers:** A mandatory **Canopy Ridge** overlook (open sky + Shield vista) and an optional **Tideglass Beach** rest node (food, banter, and a rare moment of calm).

## World 3: The Spire (Sector 0 - The Surface)
*   **The Motivation:** *The Astra* can fly low between sectors, but internal curtains isolate the Foundry and the **outer Planetary Shield** still seals orbit. The crew needs the Spire's Lens to read phase structures and find a timing seam through the internal curtain.
*   **The Solution:** The Lens grants Scan. Its instruments match the carrier pattern in Nova's Fork scar and expose the moving seam into Sector 7. The mark permits a dangerous handshake, not mastery.
*   **The Heist:** Infiltrate the Penthouse to steal the Lens.
*   **Breathers:** A Lower City **Night Market** detour (optional) and an Upper City **Skypark** (mandatory) to show Dominion’s “utopia” as a costume.
*   **The Climax (The Audit):** Thorne anticipates the theft, but she is interrupted by a holographic appearance from **Arden Vale**. Vale dismisses the party as "inefficient noise" and undermines Thorne's authority, establishing his dominance. Thorne responds by venting the room, forcing a desperate escape. Nova has to use the Lens's "True Sight" to find a dangerous exit (blowing out a window) to reach the ship. Thorne is merely evaded, and the power struggle between her and Vale is exposed.
*   **The Cost:** The Lens handshake exposes Nova's Fork protocol scar to the compliance network. Alarms rise early, and the crew has to improvise.

## World 4: The Foundry (Geo-Thermal Plant)
*   **The Goal:** The *Astra* needs a **Deep-Core Engine** to reach the Orbital Ring (breach velocity) and **Phase-Cutter Weapons** to survive the ascent.
*   **Gh0st's Arc:** This is his birthplace. He confronts the trauma of being a weapon.
*   **The Quiet (Cooling Springs):** Gh0st explains ELARA PROTOCOL to Nova and gives her his override key—trust built through action, not fate.
*   **The Sabotage:** Stealing the Deep-Core Engine physically destabilizes the Foundry. This isn't just scavenging; it's an act of destruction that halts the production of the Ascension Cradle.
*   **The Cost:** The damaged Anvil cradle detects Nova's protocol scar and amplifies noisy input. Her first Construct collapses because the signal and hardware are unstable.
*   **Stinger:** After Gh0st’s victory, **Lt. Arden Vale** executes a compliance coup, terminates assets, and relieves Director **Mara Thorne**—arming the **Soloist** variant of Project Harmony.

## World 5: The Void (Dominion Orbital Ring)
*   **The Hook:** Boarding the Ring under Vale’s new regime—clean, quiet, and actively hunting the crew.
*   **Breather (Solarium Deck):** A false paradise route—plants, warm light, a “beach”—before the station drops back into steel and terror. **(Note: This area is locked out after World 5 begins).**
*   **The Goal:** Reach Elara and steal **The Anchor Relic** to stabilize reality, then stop Vale before he completes the Great Silence.
*   **The Reveal:** The crew finds **Thorne hiding in the server farm**, proving her "Harmony" was a product-cage. Vale is the true believer turning it into "mercy."
*   **The Climax:** Vale executes Thorne (already deposed) and becomes **The Ascended**, tearing open the Tear. Elara’s signal is pulled into it.
*   **The Cost:** Nova learns the hard way that new powers don’t mean new status—Stasis can slow machines, not an Ascended mind.

## World 6: The Source (Event Horizon)
*   **The Hook:** The party falls into the raw data stream of existence.
*   **The Goal:** Rescue the party from their nightmare loops, then confront **The Ascended** (Vale) at the center of the signal.
*   **The Surrealism:** The Source isn't random; it is composed of **distorted assets** from previous worlds. Zeke's nightmare is an infinite cubicle maze paved with World 1 Mine Access Badges. Gh0st's nightmare is a forest made of rifle barrels.
*   **The Climax:** Vale attempts to overwrite their minds, claiming them as "broken fragments" that belong to the Source.
    *   *Staging Note:* Vale triggers a possession effect (visual distortion/audio motif); it fails as the Relics resonate and stabilize the party's individual signatures.
    *   **Nova:** "I’m done running. You don’t own me, and you don’t get to touch my family."
    *   **Zeke:** "Your 'harmony' is just silence. I’m not hiding in the noise anymore—I’m facing it."
    *   **Gh0st:** "Override failed. My directive is self-determined. I am the shield, not the sword."
    *   **Orion:** "We are not ice to be melted. We are not currents to be steered. No. We are Starborn."
*   **The Resolution:** The declaration stabilizes reality. Nova uses the six Echoes to "Tune the World," rejecting Vale’s forced unity for painful, chaotic freedom.
*   **The Cost:** Tuning the regional network permanently burns out Nova's Source connection. Her identity and memories remain intact; tinnitus and an intermittent hand tremor remain.

# V. Character Arcs (High-Level)

*   **Nova:** Survival → discipline → leadership → sacrifice; wins by choosing people over perfect control.
*   **Zeke:** Cog-in-the-machine → defector → crew’s heart → self-worth; learns he doesn’t have to be “useful” to belong.
*   **Orion:** Grief-stricken relic → mentor → truth-bearer → present-tense family; learns the Chorus is reaching back, then chooses the living anyway.
*   **Gh0st:** Weapon → glitch → person; breaks orders, claims his name, and becomes protection on purpose. **(Phantom Logs:** Throughout the journey, Gh0st decrypts audio fragments from Elara via the Chime, building a connection to the person he failed to save.)
*   **Thorne:** False savior (PR) → monopoly builder → deposed; confesses Harmony was a product-cage and is executed by Vale.
*   **Vale:** Polite fixer → usurper (World 4 coup) → Ascended Soloist; tries to author reality as a single voice.
