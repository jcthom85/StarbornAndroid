# Starborn — Emotional & Conflict Map

**Purpose:** To track the emotional pacing and conflict tension across the game.
**Goal:** Ensure every gameplay beat serves a narrative purpose.

---

# Act 1: The Spark

## World 1: The Mines (Origins)
*   **The Beat:** Nova wakes up, finds the Relic, and escapes.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Oppression & Claustrophobia.** The weight of the rock and the corporation pressing down on Nova.
*   **External Conflict:** **Nova vs. The System.** The Warden, the guards, the locked doors.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **Comfort vs. Freedom.** Nova is safe here (she has a bed, a job, Jed), but she feels trapped.
*   **Interpersonal Conflict:** **Nova vs. Jed.** Jed wants to protect her (stay); Nova wants to fly (go).
*   **World 1 emotional ramp:** **Routine survival -> bureaucratic threat -> coerced punishment -> impossible discovery -> hunted escape -> grief -> launch.** Each main quest handoff should make Nova's next objective feel like the emotional consequence of the previous one, not a random task assignment.
    *   **Wake Up Call:** Nova is tired but functional; the pressure is quota, hunger, and Jed's worried care.
    *   **Shift Clearance:** The pressure becomes personal. Dominion paperwork marks Nova for recycling, and Zeke saves her by moving the system's target rather than truly freeing her.
    *   **The Echo:** Relief curdles into coercion. Boggs uses the temporary clearance to send Nova into Sector 4 as expendable labor.
    *   **Red Alert:** Discovery becomes exposure. The relic makes Nova visible to the system as an anomaly, and the colony becomes a trap.
    *   **The Launch:** Survival becomes separation. Jed's Chime turns escape into a sacrifice, and the Warden makes the launch a rejection of Dominion's right to define Nova as an asset.
*   **Cost Beat:** Nova’s first Source flare is uncontrolled—she feels the feedback (erosion/memory-skip) and Dominion sensors light up.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Grief.** Jed sacrifices himself. The safety net is cut. Nova is truly alone for the first time.
*   **The Drive:** **Survival.** She *cannot* go back.

## Cinematic: The Crash (Transition)
*   **The Beat:** The escape pod's nav locks onto a strange signal, the Planetary Shield hits them, and they crash in the Wilds.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Panic & Disorientation.** High adrenaline turning into dread.
*   **External Conflict:** **Gravity + Shield.** The ship is falling.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **Zeke's Regret.** He can't override the lock.
*   **The Drive:** **Urgency.** The ground is coming fast.

# Act 2: The Assembly

## World 2: The Wilds (Survival)
*   **The Beat:** Surviving the jungle, finding Orion, recruiting Gh0st.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Wonder & Terror.** The beauty of the bioluminescence vs. the lethality of the beasts.
*   **Breather Beat:** **Canopy Ridge** (mandatory) gives open-sky relief and a view of the Spire/Shield; **Tideglass Beach** (optional) lets the crew eat, joke, and feel human.
*   **Recorded Imprint:** When the Chime's paired beacon locks navigation toward Sanctuary, the pod console replays a stored signal fragment: Orion launching it during the lab breach twenty years ago. The pod supplies its own thrust.
*   **External Conflict:** **Man vs. Nature.** The Source Beast, the poison, the erosion.
*   **Interpersonal Conflict:** **Trust.**
    *   **Zeke vs. himself:** Zeke blames his unverified authorization/navigation splice for the crash and does not turn that guilt on Nova.
    *   **Gh0st vs. The Party:** He is literally hunting them.
*   **Cost Beat:** Nova tries to use power like a shortcut and nearly collapses—Orion forces the “focus + practice” rule.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Agency.** The Elara-calibrated Source Lock pauses one lethal action. Gh0st confirms that no command is attached, then freely turns on the Beast when it attacks.
*   **The Drive:** **Hope.** They found a ship (*The Astra*). They have a way out.

## World 3: The Spire (The Heist)
*   **The Beat:** Returning to the city to steal the Lens Relic.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Cool & Tense -> Exposed & Hunted.** Neon confidence and heist swagger should tighten into quiet class violence, forced visibility, and a rooftop escape under compliance lockdown.
*   **Breather Beat:** **Night Market** (optional) is loud, colorful resilience; **Skypark** (mandatory) is bright “utopia” that’s secretly a costume.
*   **External Conflict:** **The Underdog vs. The Elite.** Outsmarting the Administrator.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **Nova's Leadership.** She isn't a soldier or a spy; she's a miner. Can she lead this team?
*   **Interpersonal Conflict:** **Zeke vs. His Erasure.** The Spire deleted his identity, but the heist forces him to weaponize old access, old shame, and old rooms. Gh0st's Elara lead should quietly remind the player that Dominion erases people administratively before it erases them physically.
*   **World 3 emotional ramp:** **Homecoming shame -> improvised crew trust -> class humiliation -> relic exposure -> compliance hunt -> narrow escape.**
    *   **Homecoming:** Zeke returns to a city that erased him. The safehouse is not cozy; it is proof that systems forget people less completely than they intend.
    *   **The Plan:** Lower City prep should feel like turning overlooked labor routes, patched clothes, favors, and deleted records into a heist. This is where Nova becomes a coordinator, not just the person pressing buttons.
    *   **Social Engineering:** Upper City pressure is polite and sterile. The Skypark is mandatory because it teaches the player the emotional rule of the hub: cruelty here smiles and files paperwork.
    *   **The Lens:** Nova's existing protocol mark closes the Lens interface circuit before she understands its controls. This is the point of no return and should feel like confidence crossing into arrogance.
    *   **Burn Notice:** The escape is not a clean win. Vale's compliance net reframes the threat from city security to orbital authority, and the crew survives by squeezing through a tiny Shield gap.
*   **Cost Beat:** The Lens tags Nova as an anomaly—alarms rise early and the plan turns into improvisation.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Validation.** The Heist succeeds. The team clicks. They aren't just survivors anymore; they are a *crew*.
*   **The Drive:** **Ambition.** They beat the City. Now they need to beat the Sky (Planetary Shield).

# Act 3: The Truth

## World 4: The Foundry (Vengeance)
*   **The Beat:** Infiltrating the factory to upgrade the ship. Gh0st's backstory.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Rage & Heat.** The environment mirrors Gh0st's internal anger.
*   **Breather Beat:** **Cooling Springs** (mandatory) is quiet steam-and-water relief before the emotional confrontation.
*   **External Conflict:** **Gh0st vs. Rylos.** The Creation vs. The Creator.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **Identity.** Gh0st learns he is a "backup" of Elara. Is he real? Or just code?
*   **Cost Beat:** Nova’s first Construct misfire nearly kills her—mastery is earned, not wished for.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Autonomy.** Gh0st refuses to execute Rylos—choosing to be a person, not an instrument. "I am not a gun."
*   **Stinger (after Gh0st’s victory):** **Coup.** Vale terminates assets, relieves Thorne, and arms the Soloist variant—making the real big bad explicit without stealing the catharsis.
*   **The Drive:** **Urgency + Loyalty.** They are doing this for Gh0st—and now they have to reach the Ring before Vale completes the Great Silence.

## World 5: The Void (Ascension)
*   **The Beat:** The Orbital Ring under Vale’s regime. Finding Elara. Thorne’s confession/panic. Vale ascends.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Cold & Sterile.** The vacuum of space. The uncaring nature of the Dominion.
*   **Breather Beat:** **Solarium Deck** (mandatory) is bright comfort-as-product—a moment to breathe before the Great Silence reveal drops.
*   **External Conflict:** **Containment vs. Freedom.** Vale wants a perfect, unified reality with no fracture. The party wants messy personhood.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **The Moral Dilemma.** Thorne’s Harmony is revealed as a product-cage; Vale’s “mercy” is forced unity. Both are tempting in different ways—both are wrong.
*   **Cost Beat:** Nova tests Stasis against **Lt. Vale** and it doesn’t hold—proof the enemy is beyond brute force.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Horror.** Vale executes Thorne and reveals the true nature of Ascension. Elara is pulled into the Tear.
*   **The Drive:** **Desperation.** The universe is literally breaking apart.

# Act 4: The Resolution

## World 6: The Source (Catharsis)
*   **The Beat:** The psychedelic finale. Confronting the Ascended.
*   **Emotional Tone:** **Surreal & Melancholic.** A dreamscape of memories.
*   **External Conflict:** **Existence vs. Oblivion.** The Ascended wants to delete everything and start over.
*   **Internal Conflict:** **Acceptance.** Each character faces their Shadow (Negative Self) and accepts their flaws.
    *   Zeke accepts he isn't a hero, just a guy trying to help.
    *   Orion learns the Chorus has been reaching for him—his people are “home,” not gone—and he chooses to stay with the living anyway.
    *   Gh0st accepts he has a soul.
    *   Nova accepts that power is not the point; people are.
*   **Cost Beat:** Nova wins by letting go—tuning the world costs her power and a piece of memory.
*   **Emotional Climax:** **Sacrifice/Tune.** Nova gives up her power (the Source) to save the world. She chooses a messy, painful, free reality over a perfect, fake one.
*   **The Resolution:** **Peace.** The war is over. They are alive.

# Conflict Consistency Check
*   **The Spine:** Nova starts trapped (Mines) -> breaks free (Wilds) -> fights back (Spire/Foundry) -> transcends (Source).
*   **The Heart:** A group of lonely broken people (Zeke, Orion, Gh0st) find a family in each other.
